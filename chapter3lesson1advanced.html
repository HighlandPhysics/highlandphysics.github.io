Lesson 3.1 - Forces and Interactions

Chapter 3 is all about forces and their properties. A force is any push or pull that changes an objects velocity. Newton's 1st law states that an object will remain at rest or in uniform motion in a straight line unless acted upon by an external force. This means that an object must be acted upon by an external force to accelerate. That's essentially what a force is, an interaction with an object that causes it to accelerate. This is defined in Newton's 2nd law, F = ma, where force is defined by an acceleration. There are six common types of forces we work with in this chapter:

  1. Weight
  2. Normal Force
  3. Tension
  4. Friction
  5. Applied
  6. Air Resistance
